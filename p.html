About Yamaha MT 15

Yamaha MT 15 is a motorcycle with a starting price of Rs 1.68 Lakh. It is available in India in 3 variants and 7 colours with high end variant price starting from Rs 1.73 Lakh. MT 15 V2 is powered by a 155 ccbs6-2.0 engine which develops a power of 18.4 PS and a torque of 14.1 Nm. It has Disc front brakes and Disc rear brakes. The weight of Yamaha MT 15 is 139 kg and comes with a fuel tank capacity of 10 L.


The Yamaha MT-15 is the street-naked version of the sibling Yamaha R15. The Yamaha MT-15 is designed for everyday riders who want a sports motorcycle that prioritises practicality over track performance.

We compared the Yamaha MT-15 and MT-03’s features. While both have digital displays, all-LED lights, and inverted forks, the MT-15 offers more features than the almost 3x more expensive MT-03 doesn’t.

Yamaha MT-15 Price

The Yamaha MT-15 is available in three variants and six colour options. The Standard variant is priced at Rs 1,67,700 (ex-showroom, Delhi) and is available in Metallic Black and Dark Matte Blue. 

The Deluxe variant costs Rs. 1,71,700 (ex-showroom, Delhi) and comes in Cyan Storm, Ice Fluo-Vermillion, Racing Blue, and Black Metallic X. 

The Monster Energy Yamaha MotoGP Edition also comes in the Black Metallic X option and is available for Rs 1,73,200 (ex-showroom, Delhi).

Yamaha MT-15 Engine

The Yamaha MT-15 gets a 155cc liquid-cooled fuel-injected SOHC single-cylinder engine that generates 18.4PS @10,000rpm and 14.1Nm torque @7500rpm, paired with a 6-speed gearbox. The engine also has a VVA (Variable valve actuation) system that ensures good power and torque availability throughout the rev range.

Yamaha MT-15 Suspension & Brakes

The Yamaha MT-15 is built on the Deltabox frame and gets a telescopic upside-down fork at the front and a linked-type Monocross at the rear. The bike uses a 282mm disc brake at the front and 220mm at the back with dual-channel ABS for better braking control and preventing skidding in slippery conditions. The MT-15 has 17-inch alloys wheels, with a 100-section front and 140-section rear tyre, where only the rear is a radial tyre.

The MT-15 comes with a ground clearance of 170mm. It has a fuel tank capacity of 10 litres, while the seat height is 810mm.

Yamaha MT-15 Key Features

The Yamaha MT-15 comes with a range of advanced features like a Bluetooth-enabled LCD instrument console that synchronises with the smartphone via the Y-Connect app and alerts on calls, SMS, email, and phone battery status. The app also gives information like the last parking status, malfunction notification, and fuel consumption tracker, among others.

The feature list further includes a side-stand engine kill switch and a traction control system.

Yamaha MT-15 Rivals

The motorcycle primarily competes against the KTM 125 Duke. A more powerful, affordable alternative would be the Bajaj Pulsar NS200.

Yamaha MT 15 Review

The Yamaha MT-15 is a streetfighter offering based on the Yamaha R15. It comes with an aggressive design and a powerful engine, with Yamaha’s signature Variable Valve Actuation (VVA) technology. Let’s see whether the MT-15 has what it takes to recreate R15’s success but in the streetfighter segment:

Design

The MT-15 features an aggressive street fighter design which is in line with the design language that we have seen on Yamaha’s higher capacity motorcycles in the MT lineup. The headlight consists of an LED projector and twin LED DRLs that gives the bike a very menacing look. The fuel tank comes with large extensions for a sporty look. The stubby rear section is complemented by a sleek LED tail light, a tyre hugger, and a licence plate holder. The pillion grab rail, while being functional, sticks out like a sore thumb.

Engine, Mileage & Performance

The MT-15 comes with the same engine as the R15 but with a different tuning to suit the street fighter character of the motorcycle. The engine is a 155cc liquid-cooled, single-cylinder motor that produces 18.4PS at 10,000rpm and 14.1Nm at 7500rpm, working alongside a six-speed transmission along with a slip-and-assist clutch.

The MT-15 also comes with Yamaha’s Variable Valve Actuation (VVA) technology which is meant to balance low-end grunt and top-end drive. But in our tests we noticed a lack of bottom end grunt that persists up to 5500rpm. Where the engine shines is above 7000rpm, thanks to VVA. The engine is also very tractable and maintaining speeds as low 24kmph in 6th gear is quite easy.

Engine’s performance is quite impressive and when it comes to rolling acceleration, the bike is quicker than the R15. Vibrations do persist but they are minimal and only noticeable when riding at the top of the rev range.

Handling & Ride Quality

Thanks to the low kerb weight of 141kg, the Yamaha MT-15 is easily maneuverable and agile, making it ideal for city traffic. The suspension setup is very stiff and this helps keep the bike planted at higher speeds and also inspires a lot of confidence when cornering.

But the same suspension stiffness makes it really hard to ride on poor roads. Even going over smaller potholes feels troublesome. Overall the ride quality is great only on well-paved roads, but once the roads become a bit rough the rider will struggle.

The brakes are not that impressive and the lack of feedback is very apparent. The bike doesn't get dual-channel ABS and this results in situations where the rear wheel locks up and starts sliding when the brakes are applied suddenly.

Comfort

In terms of comfort, the MT-15 is able to offer a better experience than the R15 thanks to its relatively upright riding posture. The seat is adequately cushioned but is a bit towards the harder sider, this isn’t a problem in city rides but longer journeys on the highway can cause discomfort. But this isn't a deal breaker as the bike is primarily meant to be a city bike.

Features

When it comes to features, the MT-15 comes equipped with a LCD instrument cluster with bluetooth connectivity. The Yamaha Y-connect smartphone app allows the rider to access features such as call, email and sms alerts along with service reminders, last parked location and other information like average mileage and post-ride stats. Yamaha hasn’t included turn-by-turn navigation which is a bit disappointing. 

Should you buy Yamaha MT 15

If you are looking for a sporty naked bike that can provide good performance and is easy to handle then the MT-15 is a good bike. It’s not really high on features, but for most riders that shouldn’t be a deal-breaker.
